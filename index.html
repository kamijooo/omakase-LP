Our Story

The Story Begins in 1962

1962

The first generation started this journey as a Wagyu merchant. A craftsman who faced cattle, discerned the quality of meat, and honed his skills. Since then, the family business expanded to meat wholesale, butcher shops, and the food industry. I was raised alongside meat as the fifth generation.

"Wagyu was never a 'special occasion treat' for me. I have known its taste since I was a child and it was always close to me."

— 5th Generation Owner

The Turning Point

However, in my 30s, when I visited America, that feeling was greatly shaken. The "WAGYU" I encountered there was completely different from the Wagyu I knew. Cattle from Australia and America were circulated as "Wagyu," and many people smiled as they ate, saying, "Is this Wagyu?"

Seeing that scene brought me both surprise and frustration. The "Wagyu" spreading worldwide is not the real thing. Therefore, as the fifth generation, I decided to deliver the "real Wagyu" to the world once again. With that determination, I decided to open the first store in the heart of Japan, Tokyo.

Why Steak & Burger

There are many ways to enjoy Wagyu. In Japan, there is yakiniku, sukiyaki, shabu-shabu, and many other ways. However, I chose "steak" and "burger," which are not originally Japanese. Because these are dishes familiar worldwide, I thought people could feel the difference from "Japanese WAGYU" most directly.

When you compare the steak and burger you usually eat with those made from Wagyu eaten in Japan, if you can truly feel, "It's so different," "Japanese WAGYU is amazing," — that impression is the experience I want to deliver to the world.

What We Mean by "Real"

I visit livestock farms all over Japan and purchase only Wagyu that I truly accept by the whole head. My idea of "real Wagyu" is not the industrially fattened Wagyu with excessive fat that was once popular in Japan.

Rather, it is Wagyu raised healthily with meat that has truly delicious red meat — Wagyu with umami and tenderness in the meat itself, not just in the fat — this is what I consider "real." Now, among Japanese gourmets, this type of Wagyu is attracting the most attention.

Halal Commitment

From the perspective of safety, to ensure that Muslim customers can also enjoy with peace of mind, I personally select Wagyu processed at the few halal-certified slaughterhouses in Japan, and only procure those I am confident in.

For Everyone in the World

This style embodies the desire to deliver a Wagyu experience that anyone can enjoy comfortably, regardless of nationality, religion, age, or gender. That is why we create a restaurant without alcohol. For those who abstain from alcohol for religious reasons, and for families with small children, we want it to be a place where anyone can feel safe to enter.

However, our store is not just for a specific group of people. This place was born to deliver the charm of real Wagyu to everyone in the world. We want to make real Wagyu available to people all over the world. That is the wish of "Godaime Wagyu Tokyo."
